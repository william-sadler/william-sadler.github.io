Human Skills Reflection

William | July 4, 2024

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

Through the Human Skills material, I've discovered several insights about myself and my ego that I will carry forward as invaluable life lessons. One of the most significant breakthroughs has been understanding how to learn. While the act of learning and practicing is not inherently difficult, I've always struggled with committing that learning to memory and truly benefiting from each session. The human skills exercises introduced me to well-studied practices and techniques, helping me build a strong foundation for learning. This has led to a remarkable improvement in my ability to learn actively and efficiently. I now possess a powerful tool for rapidly gaining knowledge and achieving a deep understanding of concepts, which I can apply in any situation.

Additionally, this learning experience has provided me with insights into my personal decision-making processes, logic, and moral foundations. The exercises have guided me to reflect on my physical health and its impact on my mental well-being, as well as the concepts of neuroplasticity and adopting a growth mindset. These reflective practices have significantly enhanced my emotional intelligence and self-confidence. I've learned to recognize and challenge my self-doubt, allowing me to develop a more balanced view of myself. This newfound understanding has not only improved my learning capabilities but has also positively influenced my overall mental and emotional health.

Finally, in relation to my ego, I discovered that I often operate with a significant amount of self-doubt and possess a relatively low ego when making decisions or operating around others. By engaging with the human skills curriculum, I’ve learned the importance of balancing self-confidence with humility. The reflective exercises have helped me appreciate my strengths and acknowledge areas for growth without diminishing my self-worth. Overall, I've learnt many insightful concepts about myself and ego, during the Human Skills for Foundations.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Values, empathy, and self-awareness play a crucial role in both learning and programming, intricately tying the individual’s inner principles to their external interactions and growth. Values serve as a compass, guiding behavior, decision-making, and the presentation of oneself to others. When learning, having a clear set of values can foster integrity, discipline, and commitment, essential traits for tackling complex problems and adhering to ethical standards. Empathy, on the other hand, allows individuals to connect deeply with peers, mentors, and colleagues, creating a supportive and understanding environment. By appreciating others’ perspectives and challenges, learners can collaborate more effectively, share knowledge generously, and create a culture of mutual respect and encouragement. Self-awareness enables individuals to reflect on their strengths, weaknesses, and progress, facilitating continuous personal and professional growth. This reflective practice is vital for recognizing areas that need improvement and celebrating achievements, thus maintaining motivation and resilience.

In the context of programming, these attributes are invaluable. Working on projects, especially in team settings, requires seamless collaboration, clear communication, and a shared vision. Empathy helps team members understand each other's viewpoints, leading to more cohesive and innovative solutions. Values ensure that all team members adhere to best practices, maintain code quality, and respect deadlines and commitments. Self-awareness in a programming environment allows individuals to acknowledge their learning curves, seek help when needed, and contribute meaningfully to the team. Additionally, these concepts are equally important in personal development. Regular self-reflection, driven by self-awareness, helps programmers identify their learning needs, set realistic goals, and pursue continuous improvement. Empathy towards oneself and others can reduce stress and prevent burnout, while strong values can keep one grounded and focused on long-term objectives. Altogether, the role of integrating values, empathy, and self-awareness into both learning and programming fosters a robust, adaptive, and collaborative approach to overcoming challenges and achieving personal and collective success.

What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

What has surprised me the most about the Human Skills learning journey is how it goes beyond just enhancing emotional intelligence, which I initially thought would be its primary focus. Growing up in an environment that fostered emotional intelligence, I assumed the course would primarily help those less fortunate in this regard. However, the material delved much deeper, revealing how human skills can fundamentally transform learning experiences and build meaningful connections. I was particularly struck by the emphasis on cultivating confidence, fostering a positive growth mindset, and the power of collective collaboration. These skills are not just about emotional awareness but are critical tools for truly grasping new concepts and thriving in diverse environments. This insight has been profoundly eye-opening, and I now see these skills as essential for effective learning and personal growth, rather than just supplementary aids. The realization that human skills can bridge the gap between merely attempting to learn something new and genuinely mastering it has been a game-changer for me, and I am eager to integrate these practices into all aspects of my life.

What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

The most challenging aspect of the Human Skills learning for me, and something that resonated with many in my homegroup, was the substantial amount of reflection and extensive writing required. While many found this overwhelming due to the sheer volume, I experienced a unique challenge. As someone who loves to write and create stories passionately, translating my ideas and emotions onto paper is usually second nature. However, this didn't make the process any easier. In fact, it was just as difficult, but for different reasons. The copious amount of reflection and writing demanded by this course has turned into a gruelling task. Despite having a lot to say, the effort, time, and thought I invest in every question transform them into lengthy essays. This daily commitment has started to take its toll, and I've noticed my ability to write concisely waning as my interest diminishes with each paragraph. The process has become a slog, and while I genuinely want to convey my experiences and feelings fully, the sheer volume makes it challenging to stay engaged without resorting to brief, shallow answers, which ultimately would undermine the benefits of Human Skills.

Human Skills has been incredibly beneficial, providing profound insights and personal growth. However, the requirement to produce such extensive written reflections can be exhausting, especially for someone like me who tends to write at length. The irony lies in the fact that while I have much to express, the repetitive nature of the assignments sometimes leads to a loss of enthusiasm and creativity. I truly empathize with those who have to read through my extensive responses, as it must feel like wading through a novel. Nonetheless, I understand the value of these exercises and the importance of thorough reflection, even if it means pushing through the fatigue and finding new ways to keep my writing fresh and impactful.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

I believe I shared some insights about this question, in a prior question above. But to drive home a point, while I agree with the question that as students, we spend a lot of time focusing on human skills over the actual process of web development. However human skills is essential because it addresses the holistic development of a student, preparing them not just as proficient coders but as well-rounded individuals ready for the professional world. While a job can teach you the necessary technical skills to excel as a developer, companies often lack the time or resources to cultivate essential personal attributes. These include effective communication, teamwork, and adaptability, which are pivotal for success in any professional setting. Companies recognize the value of hiring individuals with high emotional intelligence over higher technical skill, because these employees are often more adaptable, better at handling stress, and can create a positive work environment, which is crucial for team success and productivity. Therefore, focusing on human skills equips students with the tools to not only land a job but also to excel and lead within their roles.

The human skills curriculum goes beyond teaching self-love and personal well-being. It provides a comprehensive toolkit for personal and professional development, emphasizing the importance of self-awareness, empathy, and collaborative skills. These lessons teach us how to understand and manage our emotions, build healthy relationships, and navigate the complexities of team dynamics. By fostering these skills, students learn to create and maintain a positive and productive work environment, which is beneficial for both personal growth and the overall success of a team.

Moreover, human skills training is crucial for developing the foundation needed for continuous learning and improvement. It instils habits and mindsets that enable us to learn efficiently and effectively, which is vital in the ever-evolving field of web development. By understanding how to collaborate, adapt, and respect diverse perspectives, we can approach problem-solving with creativity and innovation. This not only enhances our coding abilities but also prepares us to tackle any life challenges we may encounter. In essence, human skills are not just supplementary to technical skills but are integral to becoming a successful, adaptable, and compassionate professional in any field.

Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

I feel that it's rather contradictory of myself to have written my thoughts about why human skills are essential and then explain why it’s a waste of time. However If this is the general takeaway of most students, then I think an essential concept has been forgotten. While it might seem redundant to go over why human skills are essential, it's important to recognize that this course is not just about learning to program. It's about equipping us with the skills to succeed in the workplace. Employers are looking for candidates who are adaptable, emotionally intelligent, and capable of handling various challenges. Focusing on these human skills, ensures that we can pass any test an employer may throw at us, showcasing our ability to work well in a team and navigate complex social dynamics, which is far more valuable to an employer.

That said, I don't want to sound like a broken record by explaining the same idea repeatedly. While I agree it's important, I do feel we spend a significant amount of time on human skills, which can sometimes feel overwhelming with all the written reflections and questions. I would enjoy the balance of human skills over technical skills to be bias towards spending more time on programming. A concept we could explore is the practical experience through pseudo-projects and bare-bone tasks that could help us apply what we've learned to real-world scenarios. This hands-on approach would be invaluable in preparing us for the actual challenges we will face as developers.

However being in the early phases of the Dev Academy course. I'm aware that there is still far more to come and that short term enjoyment would be far less effective in my learning experience, than truly drilling the concepts of humans skills to my psyche. So to justify my personal anecdotal answer: Having a solid foundation in JavaScript, HTML, and CSS, I've found that human skills have significantly improved my ability to learn and adapt. The continuous learning and problem-solving inherent in programming are best supported by a strong grasp of these skills. As we move forward in the course, I trust that more practical examples will complement our human skills training, making us well-rounded developers ready for any challenge. Which is why I believe having to do more programming practise is a waste of time, if we never get to utilise or apply our skills beyond this course.
