My Learning Plan

William | Jun 15, 2024

Bootcamp Learning Plan

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

As we approach the end of the foundations phase and transition into the bootcamp section of the course, my long-term goals and career pathway remain steadfast. I aspire to leverage the skills and tools provided by Dev Academy to pave my way towards becoming a games developer. Although this course primarily focuses on web development, teaching us HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, I recognize the immense value in the fundamental skills we are learning—problem-solving, emotional intelligence, and efficient learning strategies. These skills are universally applicable across all programming languages and highly sought after by employers. I aim to bring my passion and skills to the video game industry, contributing to major projects and series, and continually learning from seasoned professionals to further my growth in this dynamic field.

While I don't have a specific destination in mind regarding location, I have always harboured a strong desire to travel extensively, experiencing new places, countries, landmarks, and diverse cultures. Although New Zealand's video game market is relatively small, I am eager to seize opportunities that might take me abroad to larger industries that recognize my potential and are willing to foster my growth. My ultimate goal is to make a significant impact in the video game industry, helping to create immersive and innovative gaming experiences while continuously expanding my horizons both professionally and personally.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

During the Foundations phase, I discovered a strength that I hadn't initially recognized: my patience and perseverance. Initially, I listed patience and perseverance as limitations, believing I tended to give up easily based on past experiences. However, throughout this course, I've realized that those experiences were outdated. Over the years, I have grown significantly as a person, and I now approach challenges with much more resilience and determination. This newfound strength has been evident in how I tackle problems, whether helping peers or working through my own issues. I invest considerable time and effort into being patient and persevering, turning what I once thought was a weakness into a powerful asset. This strength will undoubtedly serve me well during Bootcamp, enabling me to face difficult tasks and complex problems with a calm and steady approach.

On the flip side, I've also identified a potential limitation: my stubbornness. I have a tendency to spend excessive time on solving problems independently, driven by the satisfaction of overcoming challenges on my own. While this trait has served me well in Foundations, where I had the luxury of extra time each week, I realize it may become a hindrance in Bootcamp. The increased workload and tighter deadlines will require me to be more efficient and collaborative. I must balance my determination with a willingness to seek help from peers and facilitators when needed. Recognizing this limitation early on is crucial, as it will remind me to utilize the resources available and not let my stubbornness impede my progress in a more demanding environment.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

The biggest non-technical challenge for me during Bootcamp will likely be a combination of maintaining focus and adhering to my schedule while staying on top of the material. As someone who occasionally struggles with staying disciplined, especially when juggling multiple tasks, I foresee these aspects becoming increasingly difficult as the program progresses. While I've been diligent about keeping up with the material so far, the escalating difficulty level of the coursework might present a greater challenge in terms of time management and sustained concentration. Balancing these elements will be crucial to my success, and I am aware that it will require a consistent effort to prevent falling behind.

Furthermore, as the Bootcamp intensifies, I expect the pressure to remain organized and focused will grow significantly. It will be essential for me to develop and stick to a structured routine, allowing for adequate study time, breaks, and periods of reflection. The cumulative nature of the material means that any lapse in focus or schedule adherence could have a domino effect, making it harder to catch up. Therefore, I plan to employ strategies such as setting specific, achievable goals, using productivity tools, and seeking support from peers and mentors to ensure I stay on track. By being proactive and adaptable, I believe I can overcome these challenges and maximize my learning experience at Bootcamp.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

While at Dev Academy, I am eager to enhance a variety of human skills that will complement my technical learning and prepare me for a successful career in software development. One of my primary goals is to improve my ability to actively seek guidance and collaborate with others when faced with challenges. I recognize that seeking help and input from peers and mentors is crucial not only for overcoming obstacles but also for fostering a collaborative learning environment. By developing this skill, I hope to become more comfortable in asking questions, sharing my struggles, and contributing to collective problem-solving. This will not only help me grow individually but also enable me to be a supportive and engaged team member.

Additionally, I am keen on honing my communication skills, both in conveying my ideas clearly and listening effectively to others. Effective communication is the backbone of any successful team, and I want to ensure that I can articulate my thoughts, provide constructive feedback, and also be receptive to feedback from others. I am also interested in learning more about teamwork and peer-to-peer collaboration, as these skills are essential for working efficiently in group settings. Furthermore, developing leadership skills is a significant area of interest for me. I aim to understand how to lead by example, motivate team members, and manage group dynamics to achieve our collective goals. By focusing on these human skills, I believe I will become a more well-rounded professional, capable of contributing meaningfully to any project or team I am part of.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

My primary expectation from the Bootcamp team is that they possess extensive experience in running the program and understanding the diverse ways students engage within this learning environment. I hope the team is adept at recognizing patterns that indicate when certain students might need additional support. This could involve identifying moments when students are struggling with the material, experiencing uncertainty, or showing reluctance to participate. A proactive approach in these situations, coupled with a positive and encouraging mindset, would be incredibly beneficial. It would be reassuring to know that the team is not only aware of these challenges but also equipped with strategies to help students navigate through them effectively.

Furthermore, it’s important to me that the Bootcamp team actively seeks to provide help and alleviate any concerns that students may have. This includes being responsive to requests for assistance and fostering an environment where students feel comfortable reaching out for support. I would appreciate if the team could motivate and guide students who might be hesitant or confused about the coursework or upcoming projects. While I understand that these expectations can be challenging to meet, seeing some of these practices in action would greatly reduce my anxieties and enhance my confidence as I progress through the course. Overall, having a supportive and attentive Bootcamp team would make a significant difference in my learning experience and help me make the most of this opportunity.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

My expectations of myself during Bootcamp are centred around proactive engagement, continuous improvement, and effective collaboration. I aim to fully leverage the resources and opportunities provided, particularly the weekly 1:1 sessions with bootcamp facilitators. These sessions are invaluable for technical queries, progress updates, and addressing any issues or questions that arise. Additionally, the human skills check-ins will be crucial for maintaining my emotional well-being and ensuring I am equipped with the soft skills necessary for both personal and professional growth. I am also aware that support extends beyond these scheduled meetings; facilitators are available when urgent help is needed, and peers can also be a significant source of assistance. These various support avenues, ranging from in-person interactions on campus to online platforms like Zoom, Discord, email, and Slack, ensure that I can seek help in a timely manner regardless of the situation. As the Bootcamp progresses, I anticipate that the structure and accessibility of these resources will become even clearer, helping me navigate the course more effectively.

To manage my workload effectively in the Bootcamp, I will commit to a structured and disciplined approach to my learning. This involves meticulously planning and adhering to a comprehensive schedule, much like I did during the foundation phase. Keeping a detailed journal, logging my progress, and maintaining both short-form daily and weekly reflections will be critical components of this strategy. By asking powerful, probing questions in these reflections, I can extract valuable insights to discuss with facilitators or peers, ensuring continuous improvement. This method not only keeps me organized but also helps in identifying and addressing any learning gaps promptly. By following this disciplined approach, I can build a flexible yet robust learning plan that adapts to my needs and the demands of the Bootcamp, ensuring that I remain productive and on track.

Furthermore, I am committed to fostering a positive and collaborative learning environment. This includes practicing safe habits such as mindfulness sessions, taking well-timed breaks, and scheduling regular journaling and reflection periods. These practices are essential for maintaining my mental and emotional health, which in turn enhances my ability to work effectively with others. By prioritizing these habits, I can ensure that I am contributing positively to my team, facilitators, and industry/community representatives. This approach to managing my workload and well-being will empower me to maximize my productivity and support the learning and success of those around me. Through consistent reflection and adaptation, I am confident that I can meet and exceed the expectations set forth by the Bootcamp, creating a fulfilling and enriching experience for myself and my peers.

2. A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

    Strengths:

  • Passion and Intrinsic Motivation: My genuine enthusiasm for programming serves as a powerful driving force. When I'm engaged with a topic that interests me, my curiosity becomes insatiable. I'm eager to explore new concepts, experiment with code, and dive deep into the intricacies of software development. This intrinsic motivation fuels my persistence, even when faced with challenging problems.

  • Self-Directed Learning: I thrive in environments where I can take ownership of my learning journey. I'm proactive in seeking out resources, tutorials, and online courses that align with my goals. I enjoy the freedom to set my own pace, explore topics that spark my interest, and create projects that allow me to apply my knowledge in practical ways.

  • Problem-Solving Mindset: Programming has honed my ability to break down complex challenges into smaller, more manageable steps. I approach problems with a systematic mindset, drawing upon my logical reasoning and analytical skills to identify solutions. I'm not afraid to experiment, iterate, and learn from my mistakes along the way.

    • Limitations:

  • Overcoming Frustration: While my passion fuels my learning, it can also lead to impatience when I encounter obstacles. I have a tendency to give up too easily on difficult concepts or projects. I recognize the need to develop greater resilience and perseverance in the face of setbacks. Learning to embrace challenges as opportunities for growth will be crucial for my continued development.

  • Balancing Breadth and Depth: My enthusiasm often leads me to explore a wide range of topics within programming. While this breadth of knowledge is valuable, I sometimes struggle to strike the right balance between exploring new areas and mastering specific skills in depth. I need to be more intentional about prioritizing my learning goals and dedicating focused time to deepening my expertise in key areas.

  • Seeking Guidance and Collaboration: I used to prefer working independently, but I'm starting to recognize the value of seeking guidance and collaborating with others over the years. Engaging in discussions with fellow learners, mentors, or online communities can offer fresh perspectives, help me overcome roadblocks, and accelerate my learning journey. Even though I can struggle a little to open up in an online environment compared to a real world environment.
