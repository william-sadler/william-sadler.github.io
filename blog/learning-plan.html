My Learning Plan

William | Jun 15, 2024

1. What is your long term goal or career pathway?

My personal long term goal is to develop video games. I envision myself working within a collaborative team, where I can learn from experienced professionals and share my own my passion for game design, programming, and storytelling. I was hoping that by attending the Dev Academy course, I could build upon my knowledge and help me reach into that side of the industry.

2. A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

    Strengths:

  • Passion and Intrinsic Motivation: My genuine enthusiasm for programming serves as a powerful driving force. When I'm engaged with a topic that interests me, my curiosity becomes insatiable. I'm eager to explore new concepts, experiment with code, and dive deep into the intricacies of software development. This intrinsic motivation fuels my persistence, even when faced with challenging problems.

  • Self-Directed Learning: I thrive in environments where I can take ownership of my learning journey. I'm proactive in seeking out resources, tutorials, and online courses that align with my goals. I enjoy the freedom to set my own pace, explore topics that spark my interest, and create projects that allow me to apply my knowledge in practical ways.

  • Problem-Solving Mindset: Programming has honed my ability to break down complex challenges into smaller, more manageable steps. I approach problems with a systematic mindset, drawing upon my logical reasoning and analytical skills to identify solutions. I'm not afraid to experiment, iterate, and learn from my mistakes along the way.

    • Limitations:

  • Overcoming Frustration: While my passion fuels my learning, it can also lead to impatience when I encounter obstacles. I have a tendency to give up too easily on difficult concepts or projects. I recognize the need to develop greater resilience and perseverance in the face of setbacks. Learning to embrace challenges as opportunities for growth will be crucial for my continued development.

  • Balancing Breadth and Depth: My enthusiasm often leads me to explore a wide range of topics within programming. While this breadth of knowledge is valuable, I sometimes struggle to strike the right balance between exploring new areas and mastering specific skills in depth. I need to be more intentional about prioritizing my learning goals and dedicating focused time to deepening my expertise in key areas.

  • Seeking Guidance and Collaboration: I used to prefer working independently, but I'm starting to recognize the value of seeking guidance and collaborating with others over the years. Engaging in discussions with fellow learners, mentors, or online communities can offer fresh perspectives, help me overcome roadblocks, and accelerate my learning journey. Even though I can struggle a little to open up in an online environment compared to a real world environment.

3. What skills (non-technical/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I believe there are several non-technical skills I could develop to enhance my learning journey and overall effectiveness while at Dev Academy. These Include:

    Teamwork and Collaboration: While I've traditionally focused on independent learning, I recognize the immense value of collaboration. I'd like to cultivate my ability to:

  • Communicate Effectively: Clearly articulate my ideas, actively listen to others, and provide constructive feedback.

  • Build Relationships: Establish rapport with team members, foster trust, and create a positive, supportive atmosphere.

  • Navigate Conflict: Address disagreements constructively, find common ground, and work towards mutually beneficial solutions.

  • Share Knowledge: Contribute my expertise to the team, learn from others, and leverage collective knowledge to achieve shared goals.

    • Learning Strategies and Time Management: I'm eager to refine my approach to learning and better manage my time. Specifically, I want to:

  • Set SMART Goals: Define specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound goals to guide my learning journey.

  • Prioritize Effectively: Identify the most impactful areas of focus and allocate my time accordingly.

  • Avoid Analysis Paralysis: Recognize when I'm getting bogged down in details and refocus on the bigger picture.

  • Balance Breadth and Depth: Ensure I'm both exploring new areas of interest and deepening my expertise in key skills.

4. A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme. This should include:

  • An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, Facilitators and industry/community representatives

    To work productively and safely with others in this learning environment, I will actively cultivate a growth mindset! This means embracing the challenges as opportunities for learning, seeking feedback openly, and viewing mistakes as stepping stones towards improvement. By maintaining a positive attitude and focusing on solutions, I will contribute to a supportive and encouraging atmosphere for both myself and my peers.

    I recognize the importance of clear communication and respectful collaboration. I will actively listen to others' perspectives, articulate my own ideas clearly, and work collaboratively to achieve a shared goals. I will respect the expertise of facilitators and industry representatives, seeking their guidance when needed and contributing my own knowledge to help enrich the learning experience for everyone.

    Additionally, I will try prioritize my well-being and maintain a healthy work-life balance. This includes setting realistic expectations, managing my time effectively, and taking breaks when needed to avoid burnout. By taking care of myself, I will be better equipped to engage fully in the learning process, contribute positively to the group, and maintain a safe and productive environment for all involved.

  • What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you?

    My first instinct is to take a step back and gain perspective. I remind myself of the bigger picture, reassess my priorities, and break down challenges into smaller, more manageable tasks. Maintaining a growth mindset is crucial during these times. I actively seek out learning opportunities, even in setbacks, and remind myself that obstacles are simply stepping stones to greater knowledge and resilience.

    However, I also recognize the importance of knowing when to ask for help. Whether it's seeking guidance from a mentor, collaborating with a peer, or simply talking through my challenges with a trusted friend, external support can be invaluable.

5. A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

To ensure timely assistance, I'll proactively seek help from a variety of sources. I'll leverage Dev Academy's online communities like Discord, Slack or Github to connect with fellow learners and experienced programmers who can hopefully offer insights and guidance. Additionally, I'll utilize available resources such as tutorials, documentation, and help forums to clarify concepts and troubleshoot issues.

When online resources prove insufficient, I won't hesitate to reach out to facilitators, instructors, or mentors for personalized assistance. I'll also ask from friends and family who have expertise in relevant areas. By combining these diverse avenues for support, I'm confident in my ability to overcome challenges promptly and commit to my learning journey effectively.

If all else fails, I'll probably turn to our AI overlords and hope they are kind to me this day to provide a somewhat legible answer to any of my unanswerable questions!

6. A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team

My expectations for the Facilitation team, primarily center around creating a supportive and stimulating learning environment! I value friendly and positive interactions that foster a sense of trust and encourage open communication. I believe constructive feedback is essential for growth, so I expect the team to offer insightful guidance on my mistakes and areas for improvement. :D

Furthermore, I anticipate the Facilitation team to play an active role in my learning journey by posing thought-provoking questions and challenges. I hope they will guide me towards solutions rather than simply providing answers, thereby empowering me to develop critical thinking and problem-solving skills. I am confident that a collaborative and engaging approach from the team will enhance my overall learning experience.

7. Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things. For example, you might have whānau commitments like school drop-offs. It's important to plan these in.

Currently, my schedule is very flexible and free of any regular commitments that would conflict with the course. I have the ability to adapt my learning schedule to accommodate course requirements when needed. While I may occasionally have prior engagements throughout the week, such as family events or appointments, these are typically planned well in advance and can be easily worked around.

In the unlikely event that a commitment arises that could impact my availability or that of another learner, I'm committed to open communication. I would promptly notify the facilitator or the affected learner to discuss potential solutions and ensure minimal disruption to the learning process. Whether it's a family obligation, medical need, or personal matter, I believe proactive communication and collaboration can effectively address any scheduling conflicts.
