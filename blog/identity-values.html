My Identity, Values, and Strengths

William | Jun 15, 2024

1. Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

Ethical decision-making is a difficult process. Weighing values and decisions based on your morals can be a rather emotional, and sometimes even illogical, decision-making process. An example in my personal life would have been with a relationship. Morally, I believe in chance, loyalty, truth, and action over words. My ethical decision to stay in the relationship caused great pain for myself, due to illogical and emotional experiences that weighed my morals far above my own mental health. Reflecting back on that situation, I realize that my morals shouldn't be applied to every individual. That realization helped me grow deeply as a person. While in some cases, sticking to what you believe in as right or wrong is a powerful decider in life, reality loves to stick around in a big gray area, where ethical decision-making never has a black or white answer and always teeters on being unclear.

2. Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

I've grown up around people with open-minded and reflective personalities who respect those close to them and always seek a positive attitude. I've definitely been influenced toward a growth mindset over the years, well before Dev Academy. I understand that everyone is different, and expecting the same value from every conversation is a notion that should never be considered. I feel the best way to express my values and identity is to meet the person behind the story, understanding that words can only speak so loud compared to actions.

3. Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

I have a strong growth mindset, am a quick learner and can easily relate and connect new learning to prior experiences. I'm also good at building habits and enjoy chatting with and helping others.

4. Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

I easily get overwhelmed. I might be good at building a habit, but that's different from a routine. If I step too far away from my schedule, it can be difficult to keep on track. I get easily paranoid. I can very easily misunderstand a conversation or topic and I tend to lack motivation.

5. Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

My experience working with others or in a team can vary dramatically, depending on the individual I'm working with. I'm a bit of a social chameleon; I don't have a major preference. However, I'm always reflecting on my experiences, especially working with another. Situations like being in a work environment with a team tend to work more like clockwork. It's less about managing the individuals, in my experience, than providing a positive reflection in others' minds of yourself to boost morale and improve team spirit. Generally, if tension arises, that tension is addressed. If you have a problem with a teammate, or someone has an issue with you, you address that problem and work through to solve it.

Most of the time, being a people person is really important for this skill, because arguing with someone about an issue will result in either nothing changing or it becoming worse. So solving a problem with another is usually a collaborative solution: "What can I do to solve this issue?" "What would benefit you the most to help you solve this problem?" These are useful phrases I would use in solving a problem. Never make it about the person, always put the pressure on you, as it gives the person the ability to control the solution and find their own answer and, in some cases, put some pressure on themselves.

For individual work, it's a similar process. If I have tension in the process, I'll ask myself if there is a solution, and if there isn't, I'll ask them if there is something they need from me to solve the tension, and rinse and repeat.
